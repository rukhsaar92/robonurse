Possible Causes

Chest pain can be caused by anything from muscle pain to a heart attack and should never be ignored. Most chest pain is not heart-related and isn't a sign of a life-threatening problem. This information should give you an idea of whether these conditions may be causing your chest pain:

Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease (GORD)
Bone or muscle problems
Anxiety
Panic attacks
Lung conditions.

Top 10 solutions

Most people who have been diagnosed with angina have a pattern to their angina attacks that they can recognize.You may be able to control how much your angina bothers you by making changes in your lifestyle. You may find it helpful to:

1. Avoid strenuous activities that bring on angina.
2. Eat balanced, nutritious meals. Try to limit the amount of fats and fatty foods you eat.
3. Maintain a healthy weight.
4. Limit the amount of alcohol you drink. Don't drink more than 2 alcoholic drinks a day if you are a man, or 1 alcoholic drink a day if you are a woman.
5. Do not smoke or use other tobacco products.
6. Reduce stress.
7. Control your blood pressure with diet and medicine.
8. Avoid extremely cold or hot environments.
9. Follow the exercise or activity program you and your doctor developed.
10.Other home remedies include garlic, ginger root,tumeric,hibiscus tea and even pomegranate juice.
